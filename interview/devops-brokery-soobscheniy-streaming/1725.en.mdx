# What delivery guarantees does Kafka offer?

## In short

Kafka does not ship a single "magic" guarantee: delivery semantics come from producer settings, broker replication, and when the consumer commits offsets. In practice you choose among at-most-once, at-least-once, and exactly-once, trading loss, duplicates, and transaction cost.

## How to answer in an interview

- I name three semantics: at-most-once (may lose, no duplicates), at-least-once (no loss, repeats possible), exactly-once (once end-to-end, heavier and not always worth it).
- I explain that the producer sets `acks` and idempotence, while the consumer decides whether to commit offsets before or after processing.
- For financial flows I usually pick at-least-once plus an idempotent consumer or key-based dedup rather than blindly enabling EOS.
- I add honestly: Kafka exactly-once works with producer transactions plus read-process-write in one transaction; for simple fire-and-forget logging, at-most-once is enough.

## How it works

**At-most-once.** The producer sends without waiting for acknowledgment (`acks=0`); the consumer commits the offset before processing. A message can vanish on failure, but there are no replays and latency stays low. Good for metrics and telemetry where missing a point is acceptable.

**At-least-once.** The producer waits for ack from the leader or all ISR (`acks=1` / `all`); the consumer commits after successful handling. If it crashes between processing and commit, the message arrives again. Duplicates are handled with idempotent handlers or dedup keys in storage.

**Exactly-once.** Enable an idempotent producer (`enable.idempotence=true`), transactions (`transactional.id`), and a consumer with `isolation.level=read_committed`. The broker and transaction coordinator make writes and offset commits atomic inside the transaction.

Minimal setup via environment variables before starting the client:

```bash
export KAFKA_BOOTSTRAP="kafka-1:9092,kafka-2:9092"

# at-most-once
export KAFKA_ACKS=0
export KAFKA_ENABLE_IDEMPOTENCE=false

# at-least-once (typical prod)
export KAFKA_ACKS=all
export KAFKA_ENABLE_IDEMPOTENCE=true

# exactly-once (unique transactional.id per instance)
export KAFKA_TRANSACTIONAL_ID="billing-writer-$(hostname)-001"
export KAFKA_ISOLATION_LEVEL=read_committed
```

On the consumer side, committing offsets after business logic turns "at least once" into a controlled model: repeats are possible, loss with `acks=all` is unlikely.

## Pitfalls

- **Commit before process.** Accidental at-most-once: offset marked read while the handler crashed.
- **Duplicates without a plan.** At-least-once without idempotency keys leads to double charges and duplicate orders.
- **EOS and external side effects.** A Kafka transaction does not roll back HTTP calls or S3 writes; use outbox or saga patterns.
- **acks=1 vs min.insync.replicas.** Ack from the leader without ISR can lose data on failover before replication.
- **Stale transactional.id.** Unreleased ids block fencing; deployments need a unique suffix per pod.
