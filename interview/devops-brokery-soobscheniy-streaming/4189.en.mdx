# Why do you need RabbitMQ in the stack?

## In short

RabbitMQ is an AMQP broker that decouples producers and consumers through exchanges, bindings, and queues. Messages are buffered to disk; routing is flexible (direct, topic, fanout, headers); acknowledgements and dead-letter queues give delivery control.

## How to answer in an interview

- A producer publishes to an exchange, not directly to a queue; bindings tie routing keys to queues.
- Consumer ack/nack controls redelivery; prefetch limits in-flight messages.
- I use it for task queues, RPC via reply queues, and fanout notifications across services.
- Honestly: RabbitMQ is not log-based streaming like Kafka; for full-stream replay and high throughput I usually pick Kafka or Pulsar.

## How it works

An order service publishes an event; warehouse and billing subscribe to different queues via a topic exchange:

```bash
rabbitmqadmin declare exchange name=orders.events type=topic durable=true
rabbitmqadmin declare queue name=warehouse.fulfill durable=true
rabbitmqadmin declare binding source=orders.events destination=warehouse.fulfill \
  routing_key='order.created'

rabbitmqadmin publish exchange=orders.events routing_key=order.created \
  payload='{"orderId":"ord-991","sku":"widget-a"}'
```

Consumer with manual ack:

```bash
rabbitmqadmin get queue=warehouse.fulfill ackmode=ack_requeue_false count=1
```

Publisher confirms and persistent messages (`delivery_mode=2`) protect against loss on broker crash. HA uses quorum queues or classic mirrored queues (legacy). The management plugin exposes unacked counts and rates.

## Pitfalls

- **Unbounded queue growth.** A consumer slower than the producer without TTL/DLQ fills RAM and disk.
- **Prefetch too high.** One consumer grabs all messages while others sit idle.
- **Fanout without binding keys.** Messages are lost if no queue is bound to the exchange.
- **Poison message loop.** Nack with requeue=true without a retry counter spins one message forever.
- **Classic mirrored queues deprecated.** On new clusters I plan for quorum streams upfront.
