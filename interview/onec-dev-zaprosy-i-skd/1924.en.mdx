# CASE in 1C queries and computation cost

## In short

`ВЫБОР ... КОГДА ... ИНАЧЕ ... КОНЕЦ` in SELECT runs for every result row. Long branches with subqueries in WHEN multiply cost; simple comparisons are usually cheaper than join to a classification catalog.

## How to answer in an interview

- I keep CASE on simple row fields, no nested queries in conditions.
- For large classifications I prefer join to a register or catalog.
- CASE in WHERE can block index use on the field.
- Stable rules go to a computed view field or register.
- I am honest: dozens of nested WHEN are harder to maintain than a mapping table.

## How it works

```bsl
"ВЫБРАТЬ
|    ВЫБОР
|        КОГДА Ост.КоличествоОстаток < 0 ТОГДА ""Дефицит""
|        КОГДА Ост.КоличествоОстаток = 0 ТОГДА ""Нет""
|        ИНАЧЕ ""В наличии""
|    КОНЕЦ КАК Статус"
```

For a complex status matrix join `РегистрСведений.КлассификацияОстатков`.

## Pitfalls

**Subquery in WHEN.** Runs per row.
**CASE on non-indexed expression in WHERE.** Full scan.
**Duplicated CASE across reports.** Rule drift.
**Mixed types in branches.** Compile error or implicit cast.
**CASE instead of normalized data.** Business logic scattered in queries.
