# Ranking in queries: FIRST N and ORDER BY

## In short

`ПЕРВЫЕ N` limits returned rows after sort. `УПОРЯДОЧИТЬ ПО` sets order; without index on sort fields the DBMS sorts the whole set. Top per group needs subquery or window-like patterns via temp tables.

## How to answer in an interview

- I use `ПЕРВЫЕ` only after meaningful `УПОРЯДОЧИТЬ ПО`.
- For top 10 per warehouse a single global ПЕРВЫЕ 10 is wrong.
- I narrow in WHERE before sort; do not treat ПЕРВЫЕ as a filter.
- On large tables I check plan: sort vs index seek.
- I am honest: older platforms lack full SQL:2011 window functions.

## How it works

```bsl
"ВЫБРАТЬ ПЕРВЫЕ 10
|    Продажи.Номенклатура,
|    СУММА(Продажи.Сумма) КАК Сумма
|ИЗ
|    Документ.Реализация.Товары КАК Продажи
|ГДЕ
|    Продажи.Ссылка.Дата >= &НачалоМесяца
|СГРУППИРОВАТЬ ПО
|    Продажи.Номенклатура
|УПОРЯДОЧИТЬ ПО
|    Сумма УБЫВ"
```

## Pitfalls

**ПЕРВЫЕ without ORDER.** Nondeterministic set.
**Sort on computed field without index.** Full sort of millions of rows.
**Top within group with one ПЕРВЫЕ.** Wrong business logic.
**ORDER in subquery without limit.** Still heavy sort.
**Confusion with DISTINCT.** Different operation and cost.
