# Primary (master) in replication

## In short

The primary (master) accepts all writes, records WAL/binlog, and streaming replicas apply changes. Reads may go to replicas. Failover promotes a replica to the new primary. Classic setup has a single writer invariant.

## How to answer in an interview

- Writes only on primary; replicas are read-only or async apply.
- Replication lag between primary and standby.
- Automatic failover tools: Patroni, orchestrator.
- Honestly: the term slave is outdated; say replica/standby.

## How it works

```
App --write--> Primary --binlog--> Replica1
                              \--> Replica2
App --read-----> Replica1 (optional)
```

The primary generates sequential log entries; replica IO thread fetches, SQL thread applies.

## Pitfalls

**Split brain with two primaries.** Needs quorum/fencing.

**Stale read on replica.** Business logic assumes fresh data.

**Failover without connection pool drain.** Apps hit old primary.

**Long transaction blocks replication.** Lag spikes.

**Backup from replica without coordination.** Inconsistent snapshot.
