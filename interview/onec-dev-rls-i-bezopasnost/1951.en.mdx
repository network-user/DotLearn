# How RLS affects query performance

## In short

Each RLS constraint becomes extra joins or subqueries in SQL. More access dimensions mean a heavier plan. Reports without company filter suffer more than parameterized lists.

## How to answer in an interview

- I narrow reports with organization parameters aligned with RLS.
- I materialize allowed references in a TT once at query start.
- I avoid OR with dozens of conditions in the limit template.
- I compare plans with RLS and without in privileged session on a stand.
- I am honest: dropping RLS for speed is rarely acceptable in multi-company ERP.

## How it works

The platform modifies query text before sending to the DBMS. Indexes on constraint fields (Company, Partner) are critical for seek vs scan.

## Pitfalls

**Heavy report without parameters.** RLS multiplies full scan cost.
**No index on constraint field.** Every query filters in memory.
**Duplicate joins in text and RLS.** Redundant DBMS work.
**Privileged mode as fix.** Security hole.
**Ignoring RLS in background jobs.** Wrong selections or leaks.
