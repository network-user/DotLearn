# Agile: what idea stands behind agile development?

## In short

Agile is not a methodology but a set of values and principles: the team works in short cycles, frequently delivers a working product to the customer, and adapts to change instead of blindly following the original plan.

## How to answer in an interview

Agile is the philosophy of flexible development described in the Agile Manifesto. Its essence: value individuals and interactions over processes and tools, a working product over documentation, collaboration with the customer over a formal contract, and responding to change over following a plan. In practice this is embodied by concrete approaches - Scrum, Kanban, Lean. Work is broken into short iterations (sprints) of 1-4 weeks; after each one the team shows results and corrects course. The main benefit is fast adaptation to changing requirements and the market.

## How it works

The Agile Manifesto rests on four values. On the left is what matters more; on the right is what can be sacrificed in a conflict:

1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools.
2. **A working product** over comprehensive documentation.
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation.
4. **Responding to change** over following a plan.

Note the wording: the right-hand side has value too, the left is simply more important. This is not a rejection of documentation or plans, but a shift in priority.

The values themselves are abstract, so teams adopt a ready-made framework. The most common is Scrum:

- **Product Owner** - responsible for product value and backlog priorities.
- **Scrum Master** - watches over the process and removes impediments.
- **Development Team** - builds the product.

The Scrum cycle: sprint planning pulls a manageable amount of work from the backlog, daily standups synchronize the team, and the retrospective at the end looks for what to improve.

## Pitfalls

- **Agile is confused with Scrum.** Agile is the values, Scrum is one way to implement them. You can be "doing Scrum" yet not be agile if the daily meetings have turned into a formal report to management.
- **"No documentation" is a myth.** The Manifesto speaks of priority, not abandonment. Critical documentation (APIs, architectural decisions) is still needed.
- **Cargo cult.** A team sets up sprints and standups but never adjusts the plan based on feedback - rituals without substance.
- **Agile does not mean "no planning."** Planning exists, it's just short and regular rather than one big plan for a year ahead.
