# Are cookies encrypted over HTTPS

## In short

TLS encrypts all HTTP traffic, including Cookie and Set-Cookie headers, on the wire. That is confidentiality in transit. Cookie values are not encrypted at rest in the browser store; HttpOnly/Secure/SameSite flags set usage rules, not payload encryption.

## How to answer in an interview

- HTTPS plus Secure flag: cookie will not travel over plain HTTP.
- TLS stops passive sniffing; I block MITM with HSTS and valid cert chains.
- Cookie payload is readable in DevTools Application tab; avoid storing secrets unnecessarily.
- Honestly: TLS does not stop XSS reading non-HttpOnly cookies via `document.cookie`.
- __Host- prefix cookies enforce Secure, Path=/, no Domain (stricter).

```http
Set-Cookie: sid=opaque-id; Secure; HttpOnly; SameSite=Lax; Path=/
```

The browser stores a cookie jar per domain. Matching requests add `Cookie: sid=opaque-id` inside TLS records.

Encryption is not the whole story: TLS also authenticates the server certificate. HSTS (`Strict-Transport-Security`) prevents sslstrip downgrade on repeat visits.

## Pitfalls

- **Mixed content.** HTTP subresources on HTTPS pages do not encrypt cookies for that subresource.
- **Secure false on staging.** Cookies without Secure leak on accidental http:// links.
- **Subdomain leakage.** Domain=.corp.com sends cookies to all subdomains.
- **JS-readable sessions.** Without HttpOnly, XSS exfiltrates despite TLS.
- **Corporate proxies.** SSL inspection breaks pin expectations; document for users.
