# What is a Kafka consumer and how does QA verify event consumption?

## In short

A consumer subscribes to a topic, polls record batches, processes them, and commits offsets to the group coordinator. A consumer group splits partitions across members: each partition goes to one member. QA checks lag, per-partition order, rebalance behavior, and handler idempotency on redelivery.

## How to answer in an interview

- `group_id` enables cooperative consumption; without it each reader is independent.
- `auto_offset_reset=earliest` for a new group reads retention history.
- Commit after successful business work, not before.
- Adding a consumer triggers rebalance and partition reassignment.
- Honestly: polling too long without heartbeat drops the consumer from the group.

## How it works

```gherkin
Feature: Kafka consumer QA

  Scenario: New group reads from earliest
    Given topic audit.events retention 3 days
    When consumer group qa-audit-new starts auto_offset_reset earliest
    Then all retained messages processed once per group

  Scenario: Lag under load
    Given publish rate 500 per sec
    When consumer processes 300 per sec
    Then lag metric grows QA files capacity ticket

  Scenario: Rebalance idempotency
    Given handler uses idempotency key evt-id
    When partition reassigned mid-batch
    Then duplicate evt-id skipped second time
```

```gherkin

  Scenario: Process billing topic
    Given KafkaConsumer subscribes billing-events group invoice-workers
    When poll returns record value invoice paid
    Then downstream DB status PAID and offset committed
```

## Pitfalls

- **enable.auto.commit=true by default.** Loss on crash after poll.
- **One consumer in test, ten in prod.** Different partition assignment.
- **Read without side effect check.** Offset moves, no data.
- **max.poll.records ignored.** OOM on fat payloads.
- **Different group_id staging/prod.** Incomparable lag metrics.
