# How does Kafka work: producer, topic, partition, consumer for QA?

## In short

A producer writes records to a topic; the topic splits into partitions for parallelism. Each record gets an offset within a partition. A consumer group assigns partitions across instances: one partition is read by one consumer in the group at a time. QA validates delivery, key order, lag, and rebalance behavior.

## How to answer in an interview

- The producer picks a partition by key hash or round-robin.
- A topic is a log, not a delete-on-read queue: retention is time/size based.
- Consumers commit offsets after processing; on failure they resume from the last commit.
- Metrics: consumer lag, under-replicated partitions.
- Honestly: without a partition key, order among related events is not guaranteed.

## Pitfalls

- **Confuse topic and consumer group.** Different groups read one topic independently.
- **Auto commit before processing.** Loss on crash between read and business logic.
- **One partition under load.** Bottleneck when scaling producers.
- **Test without key.** Random order; bug “does not reproduce”.
- **Ignore compacted topic.** Latest value per key, not full history.
