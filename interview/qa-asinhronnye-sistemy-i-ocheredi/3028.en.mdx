# How does Kafka deliver and read messages during testing?

## In short

A producer appends to the partition leader; replicas sync the write; ack level (`all`, `1`, `0`) sets durability. A consumer group reads from committed offsets; order within a partition holds. QA simulates broker failure and consumer lag and checks that after offset commit reprocessing does not duplicate effects unnecessarily.

## How to answer in an interview

- `acks=all`: write confirmed by all ISR replicas.
- Consumer polls batches, processes, commits offset sync or async.
- `auto_offset_reset=earliest` on a new group reads from retention start.
- On rebalance partitions move; in-flight messages may redeliver.
- Honestly: commit before business transaction completes causes loss or duplicates.

## How it works

```gherkin
Feature: Kafka delivery and read

  Scenario: Producer ack all replicas
    Given replication factor 3 min.insync.replicas 2
    When broker B dies
    And producer acks all
    Then message still readable from surviving replicas

  Scenario: Consumer offset commit
    Given consumer group billing-workers
    When process message offset 1042
    And commit sync after DB insert
    Then restart consumer resumes at 1043

  Scenario: Rebalance duplicate window
    Given consumer slow processing offset 500
    When rebalance triggers
    Then another consumer may reprocess 500 QA expects idempotent handler
```

Test flow: publish `test-evt-1`, find in `kafka-console-consumer`, match handler log and downstream row.

## Pitfalls

- **acks=0 in test, all in prod.** False durability sense.
- **enable.auto.commit=true before handler.** Silent loss.
- **Read without group id.** Every run reads everything again.
- **max.poll.interval ignored.** Consumer dropped mid-batch.
- **ISR not checked.** Under-replicated partition under cluster stress.
