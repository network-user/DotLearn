# Message delivery semantics in Kafka: from at-most-once to exactly-once

## In short

Kafka supports three delivery semantics: at-most-once (no more than once, loss possible), at-least-once (at least once, duplicates possible), and exactly-once. The choice is a trade-off between reliability and throughput.

## How to answer in an interview

Delivery semantics are determined by the combination of producer settings and consumer behavior. At-most-once: the producer waits for no acknowledgments (`acks=0`), a message may be lost but it's fast - fine for metrics where losing some data isn't critical. At-least-once: the producer waits for an acknowledgment and retries on timeout, so duplicates are possible - this is the default for most tasks, and the receiver must be idempotent. Exactly-once: producer idempotence and transactions are added, duplicates are eliminated - this is for finance and similar critical scenarios, but at the cost of performance. The key thought: "exactly once" in Kafka is not magic, it's an idempotent producer plus a transactional write, not just a checkbox.

- **At-most-once** — the producer sends and does not wait for the broker's reply. A failure between sending and writing means the message is lost.
- **At-least-once** — the producer waits for an ACK; not getting one, it retries. A retry after a write that already happened but was not acknowledged creates a duplicate.
- **Exactly-once** — the idempotent producer numbers messages, the broker discards repeats, and transactions bind the write and the consumer's offset commit into an atomic operation.

Producer configuration for each mode:

```python
from kafka import KafkaProducer

# at most once: no waiting for acknowledgments
producer_at_most_once = KafkaProducer(
    bootstrap_servers='localhost:9092',
    acks=0,
)

# at least once: wait for ACK from the partition leader
producer_at_least_once = KafkaProducer(
    bootstrap_servers='localhost:9092',
    acks=1,
)

# exactly once: ACK from all replicas + idempotence
producer_exactly_once = KafkaProducer(
    bootstrap_servers='localhost:9092',
    acks='all',
    enable_idempotence=True,
)
```

`acks=0` gives at-most-once; `acks=1` gives at-least-once; `acks='all'` together with `enable_idempotence=True` is the foundation of exactly-once.

## Pitfalls

- `enable_idempotence=True` guarantees no duplicates only on the producer→broker side. Full exactly-once across consumers also requires transactions and correct offset commits.
- `acks=1` does not protect against loss: if the leader acknowledged the write and crashed before replication, the message is gone. For durability you need `acks='all'` plus `min.insync.replicas`.
- At-least-once without an idempotent receiver leads to double processing - deduplicate by the message key.
- Exactly-once is not free: transactions and waiting for all replicas reduce throughput and increase latency.
