# img alt and title: accessibility, SEO, and tooltips

## In short

`alt` provides a text replacement for assistive tech and when the image fails to load. `title` adds an optional hover tooltip but does not replace `alt` and is rarely announced as the primary name.

## How to answer in an interview

- `alt` is required for meaningful `<img>`; decorative images get empty `alt=""`.
- I describe content, not the file: "DotLearn logo", not "logo.svg".
- I use `title` for extra context without copying `alt` verbatim.
- Search engines index `alt`; `title` on images barely affects SEO.
- I honestly add that long `title` text is useless on touch devices because there is no hover.

## How it works

A screen reader takes the **accessible name** from `alt`. If the attribute is empty and the image is decorative, narration skips it.

```html
<img
  src="/covers/react-hooks.webp"
  alt="Diagram of the useEffect hook lifecycle in React"
  width="640"
  height="360"
  loading="lazy"
/>

<img src="/patterns/grid.svg" alt="" role="presentation" />
```

The second case is purely decorative; AT ignores it.

`title` renders as a native tooltip on desktop hover:

```html
<img
  src="/team/photo.jpg"
  alt="Team at a meetup, four people at a booth"
  title="Photo from Frontend Meetup 2025"
/>
```

For informative images, `alt` must convey meaning without seeing the picture. For charts, a visible `<figcaption>` inside `<figure>` often helps everyone.

## Pitfalls

**Empty alt on meaningful content.** Blind users miss what a sales chart shows.

**Keyword stuffing in alt.** Spam hurts UX and can backfire on SEO.

**title instead of alt.** Tooltips do not appear on load failure and are not the primary AT name.

**Same alt on every icon.** "Icon" on each button lacks context; use visible text or `aria-label` on the button.

**CSS background-image without a text alternative.** Backgrounds have no `alt`; meaning must live in nearby markup.
