# Why standard configs disable auto-registration in exchange plans

## In short

Auto-registration puts every object change into the exchange queue. Standard configurations disable it and register changes explicitly to avoid bloated packages, shipping service objects, and to control export order.

## How to answer in an interview

- I explain: auto-registration suits small bases; in ERP it floods extra messages.
- Standard configs use `ПланыОбмена.ЗарегистрироватьИзменения()` selectively after business events.
- Service registers and temp documents must not auto-ship to a node.
- Allowed auto-registration applies only to stable catalogs with a filter.
- I am honest: when migrating to manual registration a missed call desyncs nodes.

## How it works

Explicit registration after posting:

```bsl
ПланыОбмена.ЗарегистрироватьИзменения(УзелОбмена, СсылкаНаДокумент);
```

In the exchange plan auto-registration is cleared per metadata object and rules live in the exchange manager module. Export collects only registered references.

## Pitfalls

**Auto-registration on documents with frequent drafts.** Every save goes to exchange.
**No registration on programmatic write.** The node misses the change.
**Double registration.** Explicit call plus auto-registration duplicates messages.
**Exchange without node filter.** Extra data in a distributed setup.
**Ignoring conflict queue.** Packages grow faster than channel throughput.
