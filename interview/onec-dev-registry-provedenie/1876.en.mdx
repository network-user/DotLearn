# Three register kinds and their role in accounting

## In short

Accumulation registers record quantity and amount movements and balances. Information registers store arbitrary data slices over time (prices, rates, settings). Calculation registers keep periodic accruals with dependencies between calculation kinds.

## How to answer in an interview

- I pick a register for the task: stock → accumulation; price as of date → information; salary → calculation.
- Movements to accumulation and calculation are usually written by posting a recorder document.
- An information register can be independent or subordinate to a recorder.
- Query virtual tables give slices without direct SQL to physical tables.
- I am honest: a catalog does not replace a register when you need change history over time.

## How it works

Document posting builds accumulation movements:

```bsl
Движения.ТоварыНаСкладах.Записывать = Истина;
Движение = Движения.ТоварыНаСкладах.Добавить();
Движение.ВидДвижения = ВидДвиженияНакопления.Приход;
Движение.Период = Дата;
Движение.Номенклатура = СтрокаТЧ.Номенклатура;
Движение.Склад = Склад;
Движение.Количество = СтрокаТЧ.Количество;
```

Writing a price to an information register:

```bsl
Набор = РегистрыСведений.ЦеныНоменклатуры.СоздатьНаборЗаписей();
Запись = Набор.Добавить();
Запись.Период = ДатаЦены;
Запись.Номенклатура = Номенклатура;
Запись.Цена = НоваяЦена;
Набор.Записать();
```

Register choice drives report models and how balances or periods are controlled.

## Pitfalls

**Catalog instead of register.** You lose price and rate history.
**Accumulation for settings.** Extra movements and heavy totals without need.
**Independent information register without control.** Records without a recorder are harder to audit.
**Mixing calculation and accumulation.** Wrong model for payroll scenarios.
**Queries without virtual tables.** Manual aggregation of movements is slower than platform slices.
