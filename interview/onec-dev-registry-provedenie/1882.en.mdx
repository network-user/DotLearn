# Three subconto limit in standard configurations

## In short

Charts of accounts and accounting registers in standard solutions are designed for three subconto per account. This balances analytic flexibility with query performance, index size, and report complexity.

## How to answer in an interview

- I tie the limit to the accounting register physical model: three subconto kind+value pairs.
- A fourth dimension usually goes to a separate information register or subconto via an object.
- In reports and queries each subconto adds joins and filters.
- Standard configs do not extend an account beyond three without architecture review.
- I am honest: bypassing via arbitrary document attributes breaks universal accounting reports.

## How it works

An accounting register movement fills subconto:

```bsl
Движение = Движения.Хозрасчетный.Добавить();
Движение.СчетДт = ПланыСчетов.Хозрасчетный.Товары;
Движение.СубконтоДт[ПланыВидовХарактеристик.ВидыСубконто.Номенклатура] = Номенклатура;
Движение.СубконтоДт[ПланыВидовХарактеристик.ВидыСубконто.Склады] = Склад;
```

The third subconto is reserved for lots, counterparties, or cost items depending on the account.

## Pitfalls

**Fourth analytic on an account.** The platform does not support it without changing the accounting model.
**Wrong subconto kind.** Posting is rejected or lands in wrong analytics.
**Empty subconto on a mandatory account.** Trial balance does not reconcile.
**Duplicate subconto kinds.** One account cannot have two identical kinds.
**Report performance.** Three dimensions are already heavy on large bases.
