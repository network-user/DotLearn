"Which database should I pick?" is a question with no single right answer. Relational, document, key-value, graph - each stores data its own way and shines on its own kind of task. Behind the storefront of names sits one core decision: which data model best describes your entities and the relationships between them. This topic hands you a map: how the different models are built, where their strengths and weaknesses lie, and how to choose deliberately for the task rather than by fashion.

**What you'll be able to do.** Explain what a data model even is; understand the hierarchical and network models as the historical foundation; see the strengths of relational databases; spot where document stores and key-value stores win; get your bearings in columnar and wide-column systems; understand graph databases; and choose a model deliberately for a concrete scenario.

**The route through this topic.** Seven sections with clear storage diagrams for each model:

1. **The data model** - how databases are built at all.
2. **Hierarchical and network models** - where it all began.
3. **Relational databases** - tables, relations, and why they dominate.
4. **Document databases** - data as nested documents.
5. **Key-value stores** - simplicity and speed.
6. **Columnar and wide-column** - analytics and large volumes.
7. **Graph databases and choosing a model** - relationships as a first-class object.

**What you need first.** A basic sense of tables and of data being stored somewhere helps; if you know SQL it's easier, but not required. There's almost no code here: the focus is on storage models and deliberate choice.
