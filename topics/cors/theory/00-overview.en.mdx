estimatedMinutes: 3

Your frontend on `localhost:3000` calls an API on `localhost:8000`. Everything works in Postman, but the browser console turns red: "blocked by CORS policy". The request seems to go out, the response seems to come back, yet your script cannot see it. You copy `Access-Control-Allow-Origin: *` from a forum, hit a new error about cookies, and loop. This topic turns CORS from spells you paste at random into a clear mechanism: why the browser intervenes, what it asks the server, and how to read the error as a precise hint.

**What you'll be able to do.** Explain why the browser blocks foreign responses and what it counts as the "same origin"; tell requests that leave immediately from those that send an extra `OPTIONS` first; configure server response headers deliberately; work correctly with cookies and auth in cross-origin requests; read the console message as a diagnosis pointing at the missing line.

**Route through this topic.** Three sections, each built around a live browser Network panel situation:

1. **same-origin policy**: why a foreign script cannot read your bank's response, and how the browser builds origin.
2. **simple requests and preflight**: why one request leaves at once and another gets a browser-sent `OPTIONS` first.
3. **headers and debugging**: configuring `Access-Control-*`, handling credentials carefully, and reading the console error.

**What you need first.** Nothing to install. Everything happens in the browser. It is enough that you have sent HTTP requests and seen the Network tab in devtools; we cover headers, methods, and origin along the way.
