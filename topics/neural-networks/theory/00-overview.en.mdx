Neural networks feel mysterious, but at their core sits a surprisingly simple idea: take input numbers, multiply by weights, add them up, pass the result through a nonlinearity - and repeat that in layers. The "magic" is that the network tunes its own weights so that its predictions match the right answers. This topic builds a neural network from scratch: from a single neuron to a trainable multilayer network, taking each building block on its own.

**What you'll be able to do.** Understand how a perceptron works and why activation functions are needed; compute a forward pass by hand; explain what a loss function measures; break down backpropagation and gradient descent as the learning mechanism; see where overfitting comes from and how to counter it; and get your bearings in the main architecture families and the idea of embeddings.

**The route through this topic.** Eight sections with interactive visualizations and small Python experiments right in the browser:

1. **The perceptron** - the simplest neuron and a linear boundary.
2. **Activation functions** - where a network gets its nonlinearity.
3. **The forward pass** - how a signal flows from input to output.
4. **Loss functions** - how error is measured.
5. **Backpropagation and gradient descent** - how a network learns.
6. **Training, overfitting, regularization** - why a network "memorizes" and how to fix it.
7. **Network types** - a tour of architectures for different tasks.
8. **Embeddings** - how objects turn into meaningful vectors.

**What you need first.** School-level math is enough: multiplication, addition, and an intuition for functions and graphs. There will be Python code, but it stays simple - everything runs on the page, nothing to install.
